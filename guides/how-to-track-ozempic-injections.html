How to Track Ozempic Injections (So You Never Lose Your Place)

Ozempic is a once-weekly injection, which sounds easy to manage — until week six, when you can't remember whether you injected Tuesday or Wednesday, which thigh you used, or what dose you were on when the nausea started. A simple injection log solves all of it.

Why tracking your Ozempic injections matters

Semaglutide (the active ingredient in Ozempic) is titrated slowly — typically starting at 0.25 mg and stepping up every four weeks as your body adjusts. Your prescriber makes decisions at each step based on how you responded: your weight change, your side effects, and whether you took doses on schedule. If your record of that is memory alone, those decisions get made on guesswork.

A good injection log answers four questions instantly:

  • When was my last dose? — essential for staying on your weekly schedule and handling a missed dose correctly.
  • What dose am I on, and since when? — your titration history in one view.
  • Where did I inject? — so you can rotate sites and avoid bruising or irritation.
  • How did I feel afterward? — connecting side effects to specific doses and dose changes.

Frequently asked questions

How often do you inject Ozempic?

Ozempic is injected once weekly, on the same day each week, at any time of day, with or without food. You can change your dose day if needed — many people confirm the safe way to shift days with their prescriber, and use a tracker like Wellion to keep the new schedule straight.

What's the easiest way to remember my Ozempic shot?

Combine a fixed ritual (same day, same context — e.g., Sunday morning after coffee) with an automatic reminder. Wellion sends a notification on your dose day and shows your next injection date on the home screen.

Should I write down where I injected Ozempic?

Yes. Recording the site with each dose lets you rotate between abdomen, thigh, and upper arm so no spot gets overused. Wellion saves the site with every logged dose so your rotation history is always visible.
