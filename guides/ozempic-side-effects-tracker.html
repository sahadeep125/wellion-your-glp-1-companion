How to Track Ozempic Side Effects — and Actually Learn From Them

Most GLP-1 side effects are predictable, temporary, and manageable — but only if you can see their pattern. A symptom you log is a data point; a symptom you only remember is an anecdote.

The common GLP-1 side effects worth tracking

  • Nausea — the most common, especially in the first weeks and after dose increases. Usually fades as you adapt.
  • Constipation and diarrhea — slowed gastric emptying cuts both ways for different people.
  • Fatigue — often tied to eating significantly less; worth watching alongside your energy check-ins.
  • Headache and dizziness — frequently a hydration signal, since appetite suppression also suppresses thirst cues.
  • Injection site reactions — redness or bruising, usually a rotation issue (see the rotation guide).

Why tracking beats toughing it out

Side effect patterns answer questions that matter clinically:

  • Do symptoms cluster in the 48 hours after injection day? Common and usually manageable with meal timing.
  • Did they spike after a dose increase and fade within two weeks? Normal adaptation — evidence you can continue.
  • Are they persistent or worsening regardless of timing? That's a conversation with your prescriber, and your log is the agenda.

Prescribers make hold/advance/step-back titration decisions largely on tolerability. Walking in with a dated symptom timeline instead of "the nausea was pretty bad, I think, mostly" changes the quality of that decision.

When to contact your doctor promptly

Track everyday symptoms, but don't just log these — call your care team: severe or persistent abdominal pain, repeated vomiting with inability to keep fluids down, signs of dehydration, symptoms of pancreatitis, or an allergic reaction. Tracking is for patterns, not emergencies.

Frequently asked questions

How long does Ozempic nausea last?

For most people, nausea is worst in the first few weeks and for one to two weeks after each dose increase, then fades as the body adapts. If your log shows nausea persisting beyond that or worsening, bring it to your prescriber.

What helps with GLP-1 nausea?

Commonly recommended strategies include smaller meals, eating slowly, avoiding high-fat and very sweet foods near injection day, staying hydrated, and not lying down right after eating. Tracking which of these actually correlates with better days is exactly what a symptom log reveals.

Can I show my Wellion symptom log to my doctor?
