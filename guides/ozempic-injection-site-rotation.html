Ozempic Injection Site Rotation: A Simple System That Prevents Bruising

Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, and Zepbound are all injected under the skin — and all of them come with the same instruction: rotate your injection sites. Here's why that matters, a rotation system you can actually remember, and how to track it automatically.

Why injection site rotation matters

Injecting the same spot week after week can cause irritation, bruising, and lipohypertrophy — small lumps of fatty scar tissue under the skin. Scar tissue doesn't just look and feel unpleasant; it can also affect how consistently the medication absorbs. Rotating sites gives each area time to fully heal between injections.

Where you can inject Ozempic (and other GLP-1s)

All the weekly GLP-1 pens use the same three subcutaneous zones:

  • Abdomen — at least two inches (5 cm) away from your belly button. The most popular site: easy to reach and see.
  • Front of the thighs — the outer, upper area of either thigh.
  • Back of the upper arms — easiest with help from another person.

Absorption is considered clinically equivalent across all three zones, so choose what's comfortable — the rotation is what matters.

A rotation system you'll actually remember

The simplest robust pattern is a zone-and-quadrant rotation:

  1. Split your abdomen into four quadrants (upper left, upper right, lower left, lower right) and add left thigh, right thigh.
  2. Move one position each week: abdomen UL → abdomen UR → right thigh → abdomen LL → abdomen LR → left thigh → repeat.
  3. Within a quadrant, inject at least an inch away from the exact previous spot.

With six positions, every spot gets six weeks to recover — far more than it needs. The only hard part is remembering where you were last week. That's what a tracker is for.

Frequently asked questions

Where is the best place to inject Ozempic?

The abdomen, front of the thighs, and back of the upper arms are all approved sites with equivalent absorption. Most people prefer the abdomen for convenience. What matters most is rotating so no single spot is reused week after week.

Do I need to use a different body area every week?

Not necessarily — you can stay in the same area (like the abdomen) as long as you inject in a different spot within it each time. Many people rotate quadrants of the abdomen plus alternate thighs.

How do I remember which injection site I used last week?

Log it. Wellion records the injection site with every dose, so before each shot you can glance at your history and pick the next spot in your rotation.
