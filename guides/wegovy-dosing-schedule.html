Wegovy Dosing Schedule Week by Week: From 0.25 mg to 2.4 mg

Wegovy (semaglutide 2.4 mg) follows a fixed five-step escalation designed to let your body adapt before each increase. Here's the standard schedule, why it's gradual, and how to keep your own titration on track.

The standard Wegovy titration schedule

WeeksWeekly dosePhase
1–40.25 mgStarter — not a therapeutic dose; lets your gut adapt
5–80.5 mgEscalation
9–121.0 mgEscalation
13–161.7 mgEscalation
17+2.4 mgFull maintenance dose

Each step lasts four weeks because that's roughly how long semaglutide takes to reach a steady level in your body after a dose change. Moving up before you've adapted is the most common cause of rough nausea weeks.

When the schedule flexes

  • Side effects at a new dose: prescribers often hold the current step an extra four weeks, or drop back one step, until symptoms settle. The FDA label explicitly allows 1.7 mg as an ongoing maintenance dose if 2.4 mg isn't tolerated.
  • Supply gaps: if you're off Wegovy for more than two weeks, check with your prescriber — restarting at a lower dose is common.
  • Good results at a lower dose: some people stay at 1.0 or 1.7 mg by design. The "right" dose is the one your prescriber lands on with you, not necessarily the maximum.

All of these decisions depend on an accurate record of what dose you took, when, and how you felt — which is exactly what your tracker should hold.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to reach the full Wegovy dose?

Following the standard schedule, you reach the 2.4 mg maintenance dose at week 17 — four months in. Many people take longer if a step needs to be held for tolerability, which is normal and fine.

Do you lose weight on the starter doses of Wegovy?

The 0.25 mg starter dose is not considered therapeutically active, but many people do see early appetite reduction and some weight loss during escalation. Significant average losses in trials occurred at the higher doses over months.

What if I can't tolerate the 2.4 mg Wegovy dose?

Tell your prescriber. The label allows dropping back to 1.7 mg weekly as ongoing maintenance if 2.4 mg isn't tolerated. A symptom log like Wellion's helps you show exactly what happened after the increase.
