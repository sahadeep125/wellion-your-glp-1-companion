The question almost nobody asks: where does the data go?

A GLP-1 tracker holds an unusually sensitive combination: your medication, your weight history, your symptoms, and often your undressed photos. Most tracking apps require an account and store this on their servers — which means it exists in someone else's database, subject to their security, their retention policy, their acquisition, and their breach.

Health apps are generally not covered by HIPAA — that law binds your doctor, not your app store downloads. The strongest privacy guarantee isn't a policy promising not to misuse your data; it's an architecture that never collects it.

The privacy test: Can you use the app fully without creating an account? If yes, your data probably lives on your device. If it demands an email before your first log, it lives on their servers.
