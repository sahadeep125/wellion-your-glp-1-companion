Weight Loss Plateau on GLP-1s: How to Tell If You're Really Stuck

Three weeks at the same weight and the panic sets in: is the medication no longer working? Usually, no. Most 'plateaus' are either normal fluctuation noise or a normal phase of GLP-1 weight loss. The difference is visible — if you have the data.

Why plateaus happen on GLP-1 medications

  • Water and glycogen noise. Day-to-day weight swings of 1–2 kg from sodium, carbs, hormones, and hydration hide a slow underlying trend. Two flat weeks can easily sit inside a falling trend line.
  • Metabolic adaptation. As you lose weight, your body burns fewer calories at rest. Loss naturally decelerates — that's physics, not failure.
  • Dose adaptation. Appetite suppression can soften as your body adjusts to a dose level. This is one reason titration ladders exist.
  • Calorie creep. Portions quietly grow as early side effects fade and food gets comfortable again.
  • The trials predict it. In semaglutide and tirzepatide trials, average weight loss flattened after 12–18 months. Reaching your response plateau is an expected phase, not a malfunction.

Real plateau or noise? The 3-week trend test

A useful rule: don't judge a stall on less than three weeks of trend data — not individual weigh-ins. Weigh consistently (same time of day, same conditions), then look at the smoothed trend line. If the trend — not the dots — has been flat for 3–4+ weeks, you have a real plateau worth discussing.

What actually helps a real plateau

  1. Audit the basics first: protein intake, portion creep, alcohol, sleep, and activity. The medication reduces appetite; it doesn't lock outcomes.
  2. Check your dose position. Still mid-titration? The next step may be the answer — a prescriber decision informed by your tolerability log.
  3. Add resistance training. Preserving muscle protects your resting burn rate — the main lever against metabolic adaptation.
  4. Zoom out. Compare against your start, not last Tuesday. Progress photos and non-scale wins often show change the scale hides.

Frequently asked questions

How long do weight loss plateaus last on Ozempic?

Short stalls of two to four weeks are common and usually resolve on their own. Longer plateaus — a genuinely flat trend over 4+ weeks — are worth reviewing with your prescriber alongside your dose history and habits log.

Should I increase my dose if I hit a plateau?

Only with your prescriber. Dose increases are one lever, but they're weighed against side effects and where you are on the titration ladder. Bring your Wellion dose, weight, and symptom history to that conversation.

Why am I not losing weight on Wegovy anymore after a year?

Clinical trials show average weight loss flattening after 12–18 months — the medication is then maintaining your loss, which is itself the therapeutic goal. If the trend reverses or you're far from expected results, that's a prescriber conversation.
